Landing Page Audit & Optimization Report

January 1 – February 19, 2026

# **Executive Summary**

Kudos is running paid search across three campaign clusters: Employee Recognition (CA & US), Awards/Rewards (US), and Brand (Kudos®). The account spent approximately $35,000 CAD over the analyzed period, generating 2,766 clicks from 49,116 impressions (5.63% CTR). Conversion performance is thin: 53.71 total conversions at $652 cost per conversion, signalling significant landing page and alignment gaps.

This report identifies the most critical issues hindering performance and delivers prioritized, actionable recommendations for the four primary landing pages that receive paid traffic.

| 🔑  Key Finding: The dedicated campaign landing pages are receiving substantial traffic but converting at under 0.5%. The gap between what the ads promise and what the pages deliver is the primary lever for improving performance. |

# **2\. Critical Issues Identified**

## **Issue 1: The 'Employee Rewards' Landing Page Has 536 Clicks and Zero Conversions**

This is the single most urgent issue in the account. The keyword 'employee engagement and recognition programs' alone has 664 interactions at $8.32 CPC \= $5,524 spent with 0 conversions. The landing page /start/leading-employee-rewards-platform is receiving substantial traffic from searchers whose intent spans 'rewards,' 'engagement,' and 'recognition programs,' but the page is clearly not converting.

* Users searching 'employee engagement and recognition programs' have mixed intent (program strategy \+ software). The page likely leads with a demo CTA without addressing the informational need first.

* Users searching 'employee rewards platform' are likely further down the funnel, but competitor alternatives (Bonusly, Motivosity, etc.) may offer clearer value propositions above the fold.

* No conversion at 536 clicks is a landing page problem, not a traffic problem.

## **Issue 2: Peer Recognition Pages Have Zero Conversions Across Both CA and US**

Both /start/leading-peer-recognition-software (CA: 33 clicks, US: 55 clicks) and the Peer Recognition ad group (CA \+ US combined: $2,100+ spend) are generating zero conversions. Key issues:

* The primary keyword 'peer recognition software' (CA Phrase Match) has $1,292 spent and 0 conversions.

* The keyword 'best peer to peer recognition programs' is flagged as Limited / Low Quality, indicating poor ad-to-landing-page relevance scoring from Google itself.

* The ad copy leads with generic headlines like 'Peer to Peer Recognition Tools' which doesn't speak to the specific software evaluation intent of searchers.

# **3\. Landing Page Recommendations**

## **Page 1: /start/leading-employee-recognition-platform**

This page is the workhorse of the account. It receives the most paid clicks (602 across CA and US) and is the only non-brand landing page generating any conversions at all (2 in the US at 0.44%, 1 in CA at 0.47%). The opportunity is real but the conversion rate is still very low for the spend involved ($10,307 combined). The keyword and search term data reveals five distinct visitor intents hitting this page, not all of which it currently speaks to.

**Who is actually landing on this page:**

* Software evaluators searching 'employee recognition platform' (the primary keyword, 672 clicks). These are HR directors or People Ops leads comparing platforms. They need to see ROI evidence, integrations, and a clear reason to choose Kudos over alternatives within seconds of arriving.

* Program-builders searching 'corporate recognition programs' (115 clicks, $1,404 spent, 0 conversions). These visitors are not yet in software-buying mode. They are trying to design or overhaul a recognition program and will immediately bounce from a product-first page. This intent needs a dedicated page (see Section 4), but in the meantime a single section bridging strategy to platform reduces bounce.

* Competitor-aware researchers whose search terms include 'recognition programs at work,' 'employee recognition initiatives,' 'recognition toolkit,' 'high five platform,' 'top employee recognition platforms,' and 'employee recognition companies.' These visitors know the category and are comparison-shopping. Absence of third-party validation (G2, Capterra) above the fold costs conversions here.

* SMB buyers signaled by terms like 'employee recognition programs for small companies' and 'employee recognition software for small business.' If Kudos serves this segment, the page has no signal of it.

* Integration-first buyers whose terms include 'microsoft teams recognition,' 'employee recognition slack,' and 'recognition app.' These visitors' primary question is whether Kudos works inside the tools they already use.

**Sections to add or strengthen:**

* **H1 alignment:** Use the exact phrase 'employee recognition platform' in the H1. The CA version of this keyword is flagged Limited/Low Quality by Google, which inflates CPCs. Matching the H1 to the keyword is the single highest-leverage, lowest-effort fix in the entire account.
